# Netflix Financial Model & Valuation

## Project Summary

This project presents a comprehensive financial model and valuation of Netflix Inc. built in Microsoft Excel. The model integrates historical financial statements with forward-looking assumptions to forecast the company's future financial performance and estimate its intrinsic value.

A fully linked three-statement model was developed, connecting the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement to ensure consistency across financial projections. Revenue growth, operating margins, capital expenditures, depreciation, and working capital assumptions were forecast based on historical trends and business fundamentals.

The projected financial statements were used to calculate Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF), which was discounted using the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) to determine Netflix's intrinsic value through a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation.

Additionally, Comparable Company Analysis was performed to benchmark Netflix against industry peers and validate the valuation. Scenario and sensitivity analyses were incorporated to evaluate the impact of changes in key assumptions such as revenue growth, discount rate, and terminal growth rate.
